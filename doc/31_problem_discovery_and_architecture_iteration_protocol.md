# Failure Card and architecture iteration protocol

Status: active only after doc 34 reaches E8. This concise protocol replaces the
former R0 controller and C01–C80 search rules.

## Failure Card entry

A Failure Card must state one falsifiable ranking failure, its eligible
population, prevalence, loss/utility consequence, and the cheapest reversible
probe. It must distinguish:

1. information availability;
2. candidate-relative response;
3. signed direction correctness;
4. user specificity;
5. learnability and recoverable signal;
6. model-family or data-specific scope.

The card must link the exact frozen evaluator, controls, seeds, data/version,
and report artifacts. A response-only observation, a narrow post-outcome slice,
or a weak-family failure is not sufficient.

## After a surviving card

Register one architecture hypothesis and one change. Separate mechanical
revision, bounded development trial, and frozen confirmation. Compare against a
normally tuned ordinary full-token base and the strongest eligible baseline.
Pay mechanism attribution only after utility survives; then test specificity,
base preservation, and independent confirmation.

If a simple objective, interface, truncation, or data repair removes the
failure, close the architecture claim and record the narrower locus. Do not
open a rescue round or rename an engineering retry as scientific progress.
