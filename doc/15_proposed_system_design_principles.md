# Proposed-system design principles

Status: dormant authorization policy. No proposed system is currently
authorized.

The new direction first asks whether a replicated ranking-relevant failure
exists in adequately tuned ordinary models. A future architecture may be
implemented only after a Failure Card records:

- an eligible population with nontrivial coverage and ranking consequence;
- candidate-relative history response, not merely hidden-state activity;
- low or insufficiently useful direction alignment under a frozen endpoint;
- user-specificity controls and a train-only signal witness;
- replication across the registered model/data axes;
- exclusion of objective, truncation, candidate-interface, recurrence,
  position-bias, and weak-base explanations.

Only then may one small load-bearing Transformer/LM modification be proposed.
The LM/Transformer must remain the end-to-end ranking core. Prompt-only
scoring, fixed routing, offline features into an MLP, and aliases of existing
attention modules are not eligible.

Mechanics, learnability, utility, specificity, attribution, numerical safety,
and novelty remain separate states. A mechanical or learnability failure does
not establish negative utility; a local improvement does not establish an
architecture contribution.
