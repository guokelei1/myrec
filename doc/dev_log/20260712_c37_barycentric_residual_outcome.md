# C37 barycentric residual outcome

C37 passed G0 and all 34 frozen label-free A0 checks, then failed A1. Its
active-set conservation law was exact and materially changed rankings, but
its candidate-specific residual did not outperform the simpler matched global
or uncentered transports. The primary's nominal NDCG@10 gain over D2p was
`+0.001673` with a 95% CI crossing zero; its differences from global and
uncentered transport were effectively zero.

This is a useful architecture boundary rather than a positive result:
candidate-relative admission, barycentric conservation, and norm bounding can
make history writes safer without making them more relevant. The only weakly
surviving signal is the shared authenticated tangent write, and even that is
not statistically validated. Continuing to adjust candidate centering on the
same KuaiSearch lineage would now create serial research-overfit risk.

The next design step must not be C37 threshold/temperature/loss tuning or
another conservation variant. Before a new complex candidate is authorized,
the shared-global signal should face a frozen transfer falsifier on an
independent dataset/representation regime. A failure there redirects the
search to the Transformer representation/value-learning interface; a stable
transfer signal may justify one new mechanism whose candidate differentiation
is learned from relevance-aligned internal state rather than hand-specified
geometry.
