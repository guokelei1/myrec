# C35 candidate-relative surplus formulation

C34's label-free terminal result isolated a multiple-comparison failure:
99.62%--99.78% of candidate/event query-tangent cosines were positive, so an
absolute half-space admitted nearly every candidate.  A fixed post-terminal
diagnostic on C34-A subtracted, for each event, its mean compatibility across
the contemporaneous candidates before applying ReLU.  No label was read.

Across C34's three trained adapters, candidate-relative surplus produced:

- 49.04%--49.27% positive candidate/event pairs instead of 99.62%--99.78%;
- 24.36%--24.83% exact-zero candidate rows instead of 0.013%--0.032%;
- mixed admitted/rejected candidates in 93.06%--93.35% of active requests.

This authorizes a new candidate-axis attention hypothesis, not a C34 rescue.
C35 fixes the threshold at the candidate-set mean (zero surplus), trains the
absolute C34 law and candidate-axis softmax as matched controls, excludes the
already-scored C34-A cohort, and promotes C34's never-materialized delayed-B to
C35-A.  Utility remains unknown until the new frozen gate runs.
