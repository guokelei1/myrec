# C60 outcome — safe interface, fixed evidence still terminal

C60 passed every exposed-role A0 mechanic.  Candidate permutation,
determinism, and zero-evidence identity errors were exactly zero; score-sum
conservation error was at most `5e-16`, no edge exceeded its base-margin
capacity, and 45.29% of adjacent edges were active.  Primary changed 11.58% of
Top-10 sets versus base and 17.58% versus wrong history.

On the already exposed 1,200-request formulation role, one-sided edge
transport recovered almost all of C59's damage: NDCG@10 was `0.575733` versus
`0.507303` for direct additive history and `0.577405` for base.  The remaining
primary-minus-base difference was `-0.001672`, 95% CI
`[-0.006862,+0.003528]`, and two of three folds were negative.  Thus it did not
earn fresh-role authorization.

Several controls clarify the boundary.  Primary significantly beat wrong
history (`+0.009284`), history-free edge transport (`+0.007321`), signed edge
transport (`+0.008290`), and direct additive C59 (`+0.068430`).  It did not
beat ordinary history-axis evidence under the same edge interface
(`-0.000578`, CI crossing zero), and its hard-edge advantage was unstable.

Conclusion: conservative one-sided base-margin transport is a substantially
safer residual interface and correct history is load-bearing, but fixed cosine
evidence still cannot identify the base's actual ordering errors.  C60 is
closed without neighborhood/threshold/depth/scale rescue.  A distinct
successor may train a Transformer likelihood-ratio for adjacent base-error
events while retaining C60 only as a fixed control and using an untouched
label role.
