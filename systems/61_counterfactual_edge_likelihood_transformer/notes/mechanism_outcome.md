# C61 outcome — trained edge likelihood is rank-inert

C61 passed contextual materialization and all G0 contracts before fit labels
opened. Three physical-GPU seeds then trained four paired 328,512-parameter
modes for two fixed epochs on 19,944 eligible adjacent edges per epoch. The
primary had finite gradients through the token Transformer, ordered edge
Transformer, factual/NULL history attention, and output head; its fit loss
decreased in all seeds. One factual-control seed narrowly failed its own loss
trend.

All fresh internal-A labels remained closed. Label-free A0 found the primary
rank-identical to base, wrong history, and same-checkpoint removal of NULL
subtraction in every seed: `0/1200` complete-order and `0/1200` Top-10 changes.
No-history/repeat fallback, determinism, permutation, conservation, and edge
capacity all passed.

Post-terminal label-free diagnostics show that the operator did not literally
write zero. Nonzero transport covered 45.45%, 15.58%, and 23.91% of fresh-A
edges, but maximum transfers were only `0.002238`, `0.029180`, and `0.000263`.
The likelihood-ratio 99th percentiles were approximately `0.006315`,
`0.000004`, and `0.000063`, with very different seed scales. These bounded
writes did not cross a single actual ordering margin.

Conclusion: direct adjacent pair BCE plus factual-minus-NULL subtraction does
not identify a stable, load-bearing base-error likelihood scale. C61 closes
before internal-A labels. No epoch, learning-rate, output scale, temperature,
margin threshold, hard-swap, seed selection, or edge-neighborhood rescue is
authorized.
