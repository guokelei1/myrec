# C76 preimplementation review

Decision: `authorize_data_free_G0_and_synthetic_design_gate_only`.

C76 is grounded in a positive, previously unopened full-token reserve result
and a frozen attention-edge attribution.  It changes the representation and
intervention locus that invalidated C25/C31-C45/C64-C75: full bidirectional raw
token contextualization is retained, while the personalized head cannot use a
generic factual state.  The protected base and structural history cut address
C04's no-history drift; the depth ledger is the load-bearing difference from
C65-C66 final residuals.

The primary novelty risk is reduction to a final hidden delta or generic
ladder side network.  Those controls are binding.  The primary scientific
risk is that earlier-layer intervention responses add no information beyond
the already strong full-token final score.  A tie closes the architecture
claim even if the ordinary full-token model remains a useful baseline.

History order is not a claimed source after token HSO found true-shuffle
equivalence.  The design gate must report shuffle sensitivity but must not
reward artificial order dependence.  Wrong-user, query-mask, no-history, and
exact recurrence remain binding.

No real label is authorized before the locked G0/design gate passes.  A pass
authorizes only a separately frozen fit/fresh-role probe; dev, test, qrels,
and the already-open HSO reserve cannot choose C76.
