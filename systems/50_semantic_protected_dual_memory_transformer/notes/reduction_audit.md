# C50 reduction audit

Gram-Schmidt projection, orthogonal residual streams, and protected subspaces
are established operations; C32 and BeliefFormer are direct internal
neighbors.  C50 does not claim orthogonality itself as novel.  Its only
potentially distinct claim is a two-memory invariant: a raw semantic KRR read
cannot be overwritten by a behavioral innovation memory, yet the innovation
may add candidate-discriminative coordinates outside that semantic direction.

This remains a simple composition with high novelty risk.  It must beat both
raw memory and unprojected addition before any new training.

Verdict: `boundary-only-with-high-uncertainty`.
