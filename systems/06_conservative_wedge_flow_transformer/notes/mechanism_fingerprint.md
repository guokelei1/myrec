# C06 mechanism fingerprint

## Identity

- Candidate: `c06`
- Name: Candidate-Local Hodge-Trusted Flow Transformer (`CLHT-Flow`)
- Primitive: history-conditioned low-rank skew flow whose candidate-local
  gradient/cycle decomposition controls a projected conservative flow
- Novelty verdict: **uncertain; high nearest-neighbor/reduction risk**
- Current execution state: CPU end-to-end structural prototype only

## Load-bearing restriction

The new claim is not “pairwise ranking” or “graph flow.”  It is this combined
restriction:

> In an LM/Transformer ranking core, every history-dependent final-logit change
> is generated by a bounded low-rank skew candidate-edge field. Its cyclic
> residual may lower candidate-local trust, but only the Hodge-projected
> gradient may supply preference direction. No other history-to-score path
> exists.

If an implementation concatenates history features into the base head, adds a
free per-candidate residual, or routes between fixed scorers, it is not C06.
The full LM uses a block-sparse attention mask: query sees query; each candidate
sees query and only its own segment; history sees query/history. Base
query/candidate states are therefore both history-blind and candidate-isolated,
while query-conditioned history states feed the wedge factors.
An autograd audit must show zero history-to-base-head gradient and a nonzero
history-to-flow gradient after the residual scale opens.

## Algebraic contracts

For `n` valid candidates and bounded factors in `[-1,1]^r`:

```text
F_ikj = (a_ij dot b_kj - b_ij dot a_kj) / (2r)
u_ij = (1/n) sum_k F_ikj
G_ikj = u_ij - u_kj
C_ikj = F_ikj - G_ikj
t_ij = sum_k G_ikj^2 / (sum_k G_ikj^2 + sum_k C_ikj^2 + 1e-12)
T_ikj = (t_ij t_kj / 2) (u_ij - u_kj)
v_ij = (1/n) sum_k T_ikj
d_i = sum_j omega_j v_ij,  sum_j omega_j < 1
delta_i = rho d_i,  |rho| < rho_max
```

Then:

- `F_ikj=-F_kij`, `T_ikj=-T_kij`, and `0<=t_ij<=1`;
- `sum_i d_i = 0`;
- `|F_ikj|<1`, `|T_ikj|<1`, `|d_i|<1`, and
  `|delta_i|<rho_max`;
- candidate-common factors and `n=1` imply `d=0`;
- no-history `where` produces bit-exact base scores;
- a potential event has candidate-local `t≈1`, while a pure cyclic event has
  `t=0` and abstains;
- at fixed `u`, replacing `C` by `-C` leaves `t`, `T` and the ranking unchanged.

Do not apply an elementwise nonlinearity after divergence: it would destroy the
zero-sum identity.  The bound is applied to shared scalar `rho` and to each edge
before divergence.

Cycle energy is computed from centered factors in FP64 low-rank moments. The
earlier FP32 difference of two large Gram terms is forbidden: when the two
factors nearly coincide it can turn a small true energy into zero or a value an
order of magnitude too large. Small-`C` explicit edge materialization is the G1
numerical oracle.

No auxiliary loss may optimize `t` directly under the current lock. Near the
fixed `1e-12` numerical floor, a diagnostic-only trust loss can create large
gradients and would change the primitive. Only the ranking loss through the
trusted divergence is authorized.

## Known reductions and observable distinction

- If `F_ik = u_i-u_k`, divergence is exactly a centered unary scorer.
- If `F_ik = 2p_ik-1`, divergence ordering is additive/Borda pairwise
  aggregation up to scale.
- With `t=1`, the visible output is a scaled centered unary potential; wedge
  non-potentiality alone is therefore not an output-level innovation.
- A single event scalar `kappa_j` multiplying `u_ij` is exactly absorbable into
  `omega_j`; that reviewed predecessor was rejected before any data outcome.
- Pure cyclic/harmonic flow has zero raw divergence, but its incident row energy
  is observable in candidate-local `t_ij`. The cycle itself is excluded from
  the trusted edge, preventing a nonuniform gate from converting cyclic sign
  back into ranking direction.
- A symmetric pre-edge energy disappears under skew-symmetrization.
- Zero flow is a valid abstention, not an implementation failure.

Every final ranking is necessarily representable by scalar scores; C06 does
not claim a larger output class. Its proposed inductive bias is the
candidate-local, symmetric trust restriction on how history can change those
scores. C06 fails its innovation gate if `t=1`, global-event trust,
potential-flow, or ordinary centered-attention controls match it.

Hodge trust is set-conditioned. Adding a candidate can change `t`
and all old corrections even when the old pair factors are fixed. C06 claims
permutation equivariance and a global score bound, not subset independence or a
tight single-distractor influence bound.

## Exact recurrence extension

Exact recurrence is absent from the first probe.  A later monotone field
`F_exact_ik=tanh(r_i-r_k)` may share the same divergence operator.  Free semantic
flow must not consume exact-matching events.  This preserves one primitive and
gives a nonnegative derivative from repeat strength to its candidate's exact
correction, but it requires a separately frozen gate.
